Requirements:

At Interplanetary Travel your safety and security are our highest priority. There are several requirements one must meet in order to purchase a ticket. In addition to the requirements, certain items are banned in our facilities. Below is a list of the restricted items.

Qualifications:

  1. Must be at least 18 or older.
  2. Must have passed a full physical examination and have proof that they are able to travel.
  3. Must possess at least 2 different forms of valid ID's for verification purposes (State ID, License, Military ID, birth certificate, or social security card.)
  4. Must not be missing more than three limbs.
  5. Must possess a passport for interplanetary travel.
  6. Must not be older than 65 years old.
  7. Must have life insurance.
  8. Must sign a waiver stating that if anything happens or if anything is lost during the trip, the company is not responsible.
  9. Must submit a proof of passing at least the basic course of space travel.

Banned Items:

Items Description

Note: If you are in any doubt about whether your item may be hazardous, you should bring it to the attention of either the airline or the security screener.
Explosive and Incendiary Materials Gunpowder (including black powder and percussion caps ), dynamite, blasting caps, fireworks, flares, plastic explosives, grenades, replicas of incendiary devices, and replicas of plastic explosives.
Flammable Items Gasoline, gas torches, lighter fluid, cooking fuel, other types of flammable liquid fuel, flammable paints, paint thinner, turpentine, aerosols (exceptions for personal care items, toiletries, or medically related items).
Gases and Pressure Containers Aerosols (with the exception of personal care items or toiletries in limited quantities in containers sized three ounces or smaller), carbon dioxide cartridges, oxygen tanks (scuba or medical), mace, tear gas, pepper spray, self-inflating rafts, and deeply refrigerated gases such as liquid nitrogen.
Matches All matches are banned from checked baggage, and strike-anywhere matches are banned completely from aircraft, but you can have a single book of safety (non-strike anywhere) matches with you in the passenger cabin.
Oxidizers and Organic Peroxides Bleach, nitric acid, fertilizers, swimming pool or spa chemicals, and fiberglass repair kits.
Poisonous Substances Weed killers, pesticides, insecticides, rodent poisons, arsenic, and cyanides.
Infectious Material Medical laboratory specimens, viral organisms, and bacterial cultures.
Corrosives Drain cleaners, car batteries, wet cell batteries, acids, alkalis, lye, and mercury.
Organics Fiberglass resins, peroxides.
Radioactive Materials There are some exceptions for implanted radioactive medical devices. Contact your airline for details on how to ship other radioactive materials.
Magnetic Materials Strong magnets such as those in some loudspeakers and laboratory equipment.
Marijuana (Cannabis) Marijuana in any form is not allowed on aircraft and is not allowed in the secure part of the airport (beyond the TSA screening areas). In addition it is illegal to import marijuana-related items into the US.
